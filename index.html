The Baltimore Cyber Games

Thank you to everyone who participated in The Cyber Games 2025 on February 22nd at Dumbarton Middle School! We hope that you all had a great time and learned more about cybersecurity and technology. More information about the Cyber Games 2026 will be available in the coming months.

What is the Cyber Games? The Cyber Games is an annual competition for Baltimore County public middle school students hosted at Dumbarton Middle School. At the Cyber Games, students learn cybersecurity and technology skills, all while competition in a fun, beginner-friendly competition with their friends. They also have the opportunity to hear from real technology professionals and high school technology clubs. Students also have the opportunity to win prizes donated by our awesome sponsors.

Who organizes the Cyber Games? The Cyber Games is organized by a group of high school students from Eastern Technical High School, Towson High School, and the George Washington Carver Center for Arts and Technology, and a teacher at Dumbarton Middle School.

Why should I compete in the Cyber Games? Competing lets you learn about fields (technology and cybersecurity) with almost limitless opportunities, meet and hear from professionals in those fields, learn valuable real-life skills in a fun way, compete and have fun with your friends, and win gift cards and prizes.
